* When the circles overlap, you draw a line between their centers
* All lines are formed when two circles overlap
* The rule is that the circles can only start from the bisectional line in the middle


Reas intentionally frames these works as instruction systems rather than fixed algorithms.

The piece is defined by the rules themselves, not by a single canonical implementation. Multiple programs can produce valid realizations. This idea comes directly from conceptual art: instructions generate the artwork rather than a singular object.

In nerdy systems language: the artwork is the behavior space defined by the rule set, not any specific frame rendered on screen.
